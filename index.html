A DATA ANALYSIS OF AFRICA'S ENERGY INFRASTRACTURE

This Data Vis attempts to take deep dive into the Energy sector of the World's youngest Continent. We analyse some of the Opportunities Risks and Gaps in the sector

Explore Data

BACKGROUND

There are approximately 2,000 power plants distributed all across the continent

South Africa and North Africa have the highest Energy production capacities in Africa, which corresponds to the varying economy sizes across the Continent

LARGEST ECONOMIES

The Countries with the Highest GDPs have invested heavily in energy production

While this may not necessarily imply causal relationships it is interesting to note that the countries with the highest GDP and per capita income also have the most in terms of energy production capacity

POPULATION DENSITY

Nigeria and Ethiopia are notable considering their large population in contrast with low Energy production capacity

Generators are a feature of Daily Life in Lagos symptomatic of inaccessible or unreliable power grids. Similarly in Ethiopia the push to build the Grand Renaissance Dam is intended to solve endemic Energy deficiancy bottlenecking the growth of the Ethiopian Economy

Generators do not return Nigerians to a state of energy independence in the form that existed before the rise of networked grids. They come to life when the grid fails and thus are fully dependent upon it.
